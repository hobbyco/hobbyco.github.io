1. Blackjack Basics

The goal of Blackjack is to beat the dealer's hand without exceeding 21. More importantly, winning often depends on calculating the Dealer's probability of 'Busting' (exceeding 21).

Ace (Ace) 1 point or 11 points (player's choice)

2. Action Guide: Hit vs Stay

The most critical decision point. Compare your hand with the dealer's upcard to make the optimal move.

HIT When to Hit?
  • Total of 11 or less: There is zero risk of busting (exceeding 21). Hit without hesitation.
  • 12~16 vs Strong Dealer: If the dealer's upcard is 7, 8, 9, 10, A, you must risk hitting to improve your score.
  • Soft Hands (with Ace): Hands from A-2 to A-6 are "free" opportunities to improve without fear of busting.
STAY When to Stay?
  • Hard Total of 17+: Bust probability is extremely high. Stop and wait for the dealer's outcome.
  • Weak Dealer (2~6): If the dealer upcard is 2 to 6, stay even with a lower score (13+). There is a ~40% chance the dealer will bust.
  • Hard 12 vs Dealer 4, 5, 6: This is the most likely range for a dealer bust. Staying is the mathematically correct move.

3. Maximizing Profits

Methods to increase your stake or diversify risk when the odds are in your favor.

Split

  • A-A / 8-8: Always Split. Turn your worst hand into two potential winners.
  • 10-10: Never Split. You already have a powerful 20; don't break it.

Double Down

  • Total of 11: Highest probability of reaching 21 with the next card. Double your bet.
  • Total of 10: If the dealer shows 9 or less, aggressive doubling is favorable.
